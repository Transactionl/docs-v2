What is transactional email and what is it for?

One of the questions we hear often at Transactionl is:¬†what is transactional email, and how is it different from other categories of email?

What are transactional emails? A definition

Transactional emails are automated messages triggered by a user's action on a website or application. These personalised emails contain unique content specific to the individual and are typically sent one-to-one. Common examples include:

  • Password reset emails
  • Account creation emails
  • Welcome emails
  • Payment invoices
  • Purchase receipts
  • Order confirmation emails
  • Shipping confirmations
  • Delivery confirmations
  • Payment failure notifications

Why are transactional emails important?

In general, recipients often anticipate and eagerly await these emails, frequently refreshing their inbox until they arrive. As a result, transactional emails play a critical role in maintaining a positive user experience and building trust with your customers, which is why we maintain high-reputation IP pools to ensure high levels of deliverability and inbox placement.

Transactional vs. Marketing Email

Two distinct types of emails exist: transactional and marketing.¬†Transactional emails are personalised messages containing unique content, sent individually to each recipient, often in response to a specific action or event. In contrast, marketing emails (also referred to as promotional, broadcast, commercial, or bulk emails) involve sending the same message to a large number of recipients at once, with no personalised content. The most common types of marketing email include:

  • Newsletters
  • Limited-time offers
  • Sales campaigns
  • Event announcements¬†
  • Vouchers and¬†giveaways
