1. Purchase a package

Any starting domain will work. This will be your 'default' domain added to your account. Depending on your package, you may add other domains.

2. Configuring domains

Adding domains is managed at the DirectAdmin panel at everest.mailadmin.transactionl.com

Adding a domain

To add a domain name to Transactionl, log into the DirectAdmin panel at everest.mailadmin.transactionl.com, then:

1. On the sidebar, click Account Manager -> Domain Setup to get to the Domain Management page. Next, click on + Add New:

2. Your screen will now display the "Create Domain" screen. Enter your chosen domain (or subdomain) name in the 'Domain' text box. Ensure Secure SSL is selected.

3. Configuring DNS

For this, you'll need to copy your SPF and DKIM records from the Transactionl DirectAdmin panel and paste them into your DNS records for your chosen domain.

💡 Tip: Your DNS records can be found at your domain registrar (such as GoDaddy or NameCheap), or at another DNS host (such as CloudFlare or ClouDNS).

If you've set up a nameserver at your domain registrar, your DNS records will be managed at the same place your nameserver is set to (i.e. If you've set your nameserver to john.ns.cloudflare.com, your DNS records are managed at CloudFlare).

3.1. At Transactionl (DirectAdmin)

1. On the sidebar, click Account Manager -> DKIM & SPF Records to get to the DNS Management page:

2. Take note of your DKIM record, this will need to be copied to your DNS host:

3.2. At your DNS Host (e.g. Cloudflare)

This example uses Cloudflare to set up DNS records. Your DNS provider may look slightly different, however it's essentially the same steps.

1. Navigate to where you manage your DNS records. For Cloudflare, this is yourdomain.tld -> DNS -> Records.

2. Add your DKIM record (from the steps above from DirectAdmin). This must be the "TXT" type. You may set TTL as either 3600 or Auto.

3. Add Transactionl's SPF record. This is:
"v=spf1 include:mxlogin.com -all"

ℹ️ For Subdomains: For example, if you're configuring yoursubdomain.example.tld, then the 'Name' field will be "yoursubdomain" instead of @ or blank/empty.

What is transactional email and what is it for?

One of the questions we hear often at Transactionl is: what is transactional email, and how is it different from other categories of email?

What are transactional emails? A definition

Transactional emails are automated messages triggered by a user's action on a website or application. These personalised emails contain unique content specific to the individual and are typically sent one-to-one. Common examples include:

  • Password reset emails
  • Account creation emails
  • Welcome emails
  • Payment invoices
  • Purchase receipts
  • Order confirmation emails
  • Shipping confirmations
  • Delivery confirmations
  • Payment failure notifications

What can I use Transactionl with?

Often, our customers use Transactionl with applications on the web such as:

  • WordPress
  • WooCommerce
  • Microsoft Power Automate
  • Bubble.io
  • Zapier
  • WHMCS
  • Joomla!
  • FreeScout
  • ..and many more!

Why are transactional emails important?

In general, recipients often anticipate and eagerly await these emails, frequently refreshing their inbox until they arrive. As a result, transactional emails play a critical role in maintaining a positive user experience and building trust with your customers, which is why we maintain high-reputation IP pools to ensure high levels of deliverability and inbox placement.

Transactional vs. Marketing Email

Two distinct types of emails exist: transactional and marketing. Transactional emails are personalised messages containing unique content, sent individually to each recipient, often in response to a specific action or event. In contrast, marketing emails (also referred to as promotional, broadcast, commercial, or bulk emails) involve sending the same message to a large number of recipients at once, with no personalised content. The most common types of marketing email include:

  • Newsletters
  • Limited-time offers
  • Sales campaigns
  • Event announcements 
  • Vouchers and giveaways

As we are a transactional email service, we do not allow marketing emails to be sent using Transactionl as it damages our sender reputation. We regularly monitor and accounts misusing our services to send marketing emails to ensure excellent sender reputation and inbox placement for our customers.
