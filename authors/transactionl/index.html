What are my SMTP/IMAP/POP3 details?*

In most cases, all you require is SMTP to use Transactionl with your application.

Email Address: The email address you created in DirectAdmin.
Password: The password you set for the mailbox in DirectAdmin.
Server/Hostname: everest.mxrouting.net
SMTP SSL Port: 465 (Recommended)
SMTP Non-SSL/STARTTLS: 25, 587, 2525 (Not recommended due to security)

If you'd like to use your own email client to read/delete error messages and bounce notifications, you can use the following protocols/ports:

IMAP SSL: 993
IMAP Non-SSL: 143
POP3 SSL: 995
POP3 Non-SSL: 110

What are your sending limits?

If you're wondering about our sending limits and how they work, you're in the right place. Our sending limit is simple: 300 outgoing emails per hour, per mailbox. You may not use multiple mailboxes for the same purpose to circumvent this limit for the same use case. However, you can (and should!) use separate mailboxes for separate purposes/use cases. If you genuinely need to send more than 300 transactional emails per hour, please let us know - we do increase this limit on a case-by-case basis. This is because our provider enforces limits in order to preserve deliverability and prevent abuse by spammers. In this example, our customer has separated their mailboxes for different purposes, so they can send up to 300 outgoing emails/hour for each different purpose/use case: form@example.com - Contact Form Notification (300/h) updates@example.com - WordPress Plugins/Theme/Core Notifications (300/h) billing@example.com - Billing Notifications (300/h) In this example, our customer is using 3 different inboxes for the purpose/same use case, in order to circumvent the 300 outgoing emails/hour limit. This is not allowed, and is considered abuse of our service: billing@example.com - Billing 1 (300/h) billing2@example.com - Billing 2 (600/h) billing3@example.com - Billing 3 (900/h)
